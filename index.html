An astronomer in Albuquerque, New Mexico

I study objects far outside our own galaxy. My research has mainly focused on rapidly evolving, compact star forming galaxies known as Luminous Compact Blue Galaxies (LCBGs). These objects were very common over 7 Billion years ago, but we don't see many today. We're trying to determine what happens to them!

I also study Active Galactic Nuclei, or AGN. AGN are the black holes at the centers of galaxies. I mainly study how we can use these black holes to create a grid of objects on the sky that we can use to orient ourselves in space. I'm also interested in temporal changes in shape and flux!
